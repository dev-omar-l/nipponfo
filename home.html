A quick introduction to 'The land of The Rising Sun'

Japan is an island country in East Asia, located in the Pacific Ocean. The kanji (日本) that make up Japan's name means 'Sun Origin', That’s why it is called the "Land of the Rising Sun". It is Consisted of many islands, the five main ones are:

  • Honshu
  • Hokkaido
  • Kyushu
  • Shikoku
  • Okinawa

With Hokkaido being in the extreme north while Okinawa in the extreme south.

Japan has a constitutional monarchy, headed by an emperor. The current emperor is Naruhito; He serves primarily as the symbolic and diplomatic leader of the country. The political leader of Japan is the Prime Minister, Shinzo Abe.

Japan benefits from a highly skilled and educated workforce, it has the largest proportion of citizens holding a degree in the world. Japan maintains a modern military with the world's eighth-largest military budget, used for self-defense and peacekeeping roles; it ranked as the world's fourth-most powerful military in 2015.

Japan is a highly developed country with a very high standard of living and Human Development Index. Its population enjoys one of the highest life expectancy and the third lowest infant mortality rate in the world! As of 2019, Japanese citizens had visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 189 countries and territories, ranking the Japanese passport 1st in the world, tied with Singapore.

Conversation skills

'Konnichiwa' means Hello

'Ohayō' means Good Morning

'Konbanwa' means Good Evening

'Oyasumi Nasai' means Goodnight

'Arigatō' means Thanks

'Sumimasen' means Sorry

'Onegaishimasu' means Please

'Hai' means Yes

'Ie' means No

'Mata Ne' means See You Again

'Tadaima' means I'm Home

'Sayōnara' means Goodbye
